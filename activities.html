Research

IMAM research will be leading to two types of audiences. First, we will do research writing to give information to the general public to promote liberal democratic values and protect minority right in democracies. Second, detail researching and publishing advocacy papers for political actors highlighting the importance of the Muslim minority issue in building a peaceful federal democratic future Burma.

Training

Training is targeting young Muslim activists and young people from various communities to promote multiculturalism and to better understand diverse communities within the country. Training sections will be covering human rights, minority rights in federalism, woman's rights in democracy, and so on. Both online and on-ground training sections will conduct with the scholars in the relevant fields.

Advocacy

Policy advocacy is needed in this transitional period and future Burma as the people of Burma were hoping for a “New” state totally different from the past. We will advocate the issue of religious minorities to international organizations, student and youth organizations, student unions, resistance political organizations, and members of parliament.

Humanitarian Assistance

According to UNHCR data, approximately 1.9 million people in Myanmar have been displaced due to the escalating conflict between the military junta and resistance forces. The intensification of fighting has forced thousands of individuals to flee to neighboring countries, driven by concerns for their safety and security. Additionally, data from the Assistance Association of Political Prisoners Burma reveals that as of now, 19,922 people remain detained in prisons.

In response to this humanitarian crisis, IMAM is actively engaged in delivering crucial assistance to those affected. This includes providing essential foods and medicines to displaced people within Internally Displaced Persons (IDP) camps inside Burma. Moreover, IMAM is extending support to political prisoners, reflecting a commitment to addressing both the immediate needs of displaced populations and advocating for the rights and well-being of those detained. This demonstrates a crucial effort to alleviate the hardships faced by individuals affected by the ongoing conflict and political unrest in Myanmar.
